Key Risk & Opportunity Summary Executive View
Top areas to act on from this Titanic passenger subset.
  • Systemic risk for 3rd‑class males: high counts, low survival, and low fares compound into the most vulnerable cluster.
  • Fare is a strong proxy for safety access. Higher fares correlate with better outcomes, suggesting unequal evacuation access.
  • Children and females benefited from policy‑driven prioritization, demonstrating the impact of explicit triage rules.
  • Data gaps in Cabin and partial gaps in Age reduce the precision of fine‑grained risk maps by deck or exact location.
Actionable Takeaways
  1. Design safety policies that do not depend on class or price.
  2. Instrument lower decks with clearer routes and dedicated staff.
  3. Use features like Sex, Age, Pclass, Fare for predictive risk models.
