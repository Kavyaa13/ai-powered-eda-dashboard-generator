Key Insights & Recommendations

Translating patterns into actions
• Risk focus: 3rd‑class males • Opportunity: high‑fare families • Data: age & cabin completion
  • Risk concentration: Male, 3rd‑class passengers form the largest at‑risk group; emergency protocols and physical access (deck / gate constraints) likely limited their survival.
  • Revenue‑aware prioritisation: A small number of 1st‑class family groups generate disproportionately high revenue. Including family linkage (SibSp, Parch, shared tickets) in models can highlight high‑value family risk.
  • Data quality improvement: Age and cabin are missing for many records. For modern analogues, enforce mandatory capture or use proxy features (title, loyalty tier, booking source) to maintain risk‑model robustness.
  • Model governance: Because the cohort is dominated by passengers embarking at Southampton and in 3rd class, validate survival models thoroughly on under‑represented segments (e.g., Cherbourg 1st‑class passengers).
